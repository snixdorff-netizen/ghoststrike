# GhostStrike Alpha Test Brief

## Project Name

GhostStrike Alpha Launch Copilot

## Brief

GhostStrike is preparing a 10-user alpha for a browser-based AI product-development platform that guides teams through a structured workflow from problem framing to concept generation, evaluation, launch planning, and post-launch measurement. The immediate goal is to validate whether early users can understand the workflow, trust the outputs, and complete a meaningful project without guided onboarding.

The test product should focus on small business founders, operators, and innovation leads who need a faster way to structure new product ideas, pressure-test assumptions, and produce actionable launch plans. Most users will not be deeply technical. They need clear interfaces, useful summaries, and visible next steps more than raw model complexity.

The alpha should answer five practical questions:

1. Can a first-time user create a project and understand what to do next?
2. Do the EMPATHIZE and IDEATE outputs feel relevant and credible?
3. Does the human gate model make the workflow clearer or slower?
4. Can users understand the difference between project ownership, collaboration, and invitations?
5. Do users see enough value to continue beyond one test session?

Constraints:

- The product is in alpha and should be framed as guided evaluation software, not a finished autonomous platform.
- Outputs should optimize for clarity, business usefulness, and confidence-building.
- The first user sessions should be completable in 15 to 25 minutes.
- The product should feel credible for founders, operators, and technical partners reviewing the platform.
- Feedback capture must be part of the workflow so product and UX issues are collected during testing.

Success criteria:

- At least 8 of 10 invited alpha users log in successfully.
- At least 7 of 10 create a project and run at least one phase.
- At least 5 of 10 submit structured feedback in-product.
- Users can explain the purpose of the approval gate and the meaning of the project outputs.
- At least 3 users ask to keep using the product after the first session.

Recommended initial workflow:

- Create the project from this brief.
- Run `V1 Package` first for a fast end-to-end artifact set.
- Review outputs with focus on clarity, prioritization, and decision usefulness.
- Submit feedback in the project feedback form under `UX`, `BUG`, or `OUTPUT_QUALITY`.

## What Good Output Looks Like

- Clear target user definition
- Specific market framing
- Realistic concept directions
- Actionable GTM and financial reasoning
- Obvious next decision for the human reviewer

## What To Watch For During The Test

- Confusing language in the project flow
- Weak or repetitive agent outputs
- Unclear ownership or invitation behavior
- Uncertainty about when to run, queue, approve, or reject
- Missing trust signals in the UI
